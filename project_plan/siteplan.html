Logo:

Name: Flavorfy

A Recipe Collection Manager to keep track of recipes, calculate costs, and manage your family's or bossiness's recipe collections.

Optional domain: flavorfy.com

Purpose

To provide an easy-to-use platform for managing and sharing recipes, with built-in cost calculation and organization tools. This project addresses two main needs:

  • Families: Preserve and share recipes across generations.
  • Food Entrepreneurs: Calculate costs, set prices, and maintain quality for business success.

Scenarios

Scenarios are questions asked by a site visitor who represents the organization's target audience. These questions help drive the content of the website by providing answers to the visitor's needs.

  • Scenario 1: A home cook wants to preserve their grandmother's recipes and share them with family members. They ask: "How can I organize and share my family recipes in one place?"
  • Scenario 2: A food entrepreneur is starting a home-based bakery and needs to calculate costs for their products. They ask: "How can I calculate the cost of ingredients and determine the best price for my baked goods?"

Color Palette & Usage

  • #F5EFE6 (Eggshell/Off-White)
    • Description: A soft, warm off-white that serves as a gentle and inviting base for the design.
    • Usage:
      • Main Background Color: This color is used for the overall background of both the desktop and mobile versions, creating a clean and warm canvas for all other elements.
  • #4D403D (Dark Chocolate Brown)
    • Description: A deep, rich brown that offers high contrast and readability without the harshness of pure black.
    • Usage:
      • Headings & Text: This is the primary color for all typography. It's used for the main "Flavorfy" logo text, section titles like "Favorites" and "Your Recipes," and the text within the recipe cards (e.g., "Ratatouille," "Lorem ipsum").
  • #5A7454 (Earthy Green)
    • Description: A muted, natural green reminiscent of herbs and fresh produce.
    • Usage:
      • Primary Navigation & Structural Elements: This color is used for key navigational components.
      • Buttons (Desktop): The main navigation buttons ("Home," "Cookbook," "Calculator").
      • Header/Footer: The bottom footer bar on both versions and top bar on the mobile view.
  • #A95C32 (Terracotta/Rust)
    • Description: A warm, earthy, and appetizing reddish-brown that acts as a primary accent.
    • Usage:
      • Accent & Call-to-Action: This color draws the user's eye to important interactive elements.
      • Logo Icon: The speech bubble part of the Flavorfy logo.
      • Search Bar: The border of the search bar and the background of the search submission button (the arrow).
  • #FFFFFF (Pure White)
    • Description: A clean, crisp white used to create separation and highlight specific content areas.
    • Usage:
      • Card Backgrounds: The background of the individual recipe cards in the "Favorites" and "Your Recipes" sections.
      • Input Fields: The background of the search bar input field. This makes the cards and the search field "pop" against the slightly darker #F5EFE6 main background.

Typography

Two fonts are used to create a harmonious and functional design:

  • Lora (The Display & Heading Font)
    • Description: Lora is a well-balanced, contemporary serif font with a calligraphic feel. In this design, it is used to give the application a warm, inviting, and slightly classic personality, much like a cherished cookbook. Its moderate contrast makes it excellent for display purposes.
    • Usage:
      • Main Logo: The "Flavorfy" brand name is the most prominent use of Lora, likely in a bold weight to be impactful and memorable.
      • Section Headings: Major section titles like "Favorites" and "Your Recipes" use Lora to create a clear and elegant visual hierarchy, guiding the user's eye through the page content.
      • Card Titles: The title of each recipe (e.g., "Ratatouille") also appears to use Lora, but at a smaller size and possibly a regular weight. This maintains a consistent, sophisticated style within the content blocks themselves.
      • UI Elements & Navigation: All interactive and functional text uses Roboto. This includes:
        • The navigation buttons on desktop ("Home," "Cookbook," "Calculator").
        • Action links and labels like "Add your recipe +" and "More recipes +".
  • Roboto (The Body & UI Font)
    • Description: Roboto is a geometric sans-serif font known for its excellent readability on screens of all sizes. It serves as the "workhorse" font for the interface, presenting information clearly and cleanly without distracting from the content.
    • Usage:
      • Paragraph/Description Text: The short descriptions within the recipe cards ("Lorem ipsum") would use Roboto for optimal legibility.
      • Footer Text: Any text in the footer or other secondary information areas would also use Roboto.
  • Why This Combination Works
    • This pairing creates a strong, user-friendly visual hierarchy. The elegance of Lora draws users to key titles and branding elements, giving the app a professional and appealing "flavor." Simultaneously, the clean utility of Roboto makes the interface effortless to read and navigate, ensuring a smooth and functional user experience.
